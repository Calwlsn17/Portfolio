U.S. Forest Change and Carbon Balance

ArcGIS StoryMap

For this project, I wanted to make something that shows how forest change looks across the U.S. in a way that is simple and easy to understand. The goal was to build a clear walkthrough of how different regions are changing and how forests switch between being a sink and a source. I kept the colors simple, using red and blue to represent fire and forest cover loss, and kept everything else pretty straightforward so the patterns are easy to read, focusing on function over form in this instance. The bivariate map shows how tree cover loss and carbon flux overlap, and the wildfire map adds extra context that explains many of the western patterns.
